The Scots College, Thursday, 19th November, 2015

ScotsFest is a showcase of young filmmaking talent. Now in its eigth year, ScotsFest is open to all school age filmmakers.
Now in its eigth year, Scotsfest is gaining more momentum as each year passes. The 2015 competition is expected to produce an even higher standard of student film. This year we hope to widen the competition even more by inviting our neighbouring schools once again and celebrate talented filmmakers. Entries close on the 13th of November for cinema spectacular event.

ScotsFest will be held on Thursday the 19th of November 2015, in the Auditorium of The Scots College, Bellevue Hill. Enter from Victoria Road. Guests are asked to arrive from 5:30pm with program commencing at 6:30pm.

Movie snacks and BBQ food is available.
Entry is free.
For more information, please contact The Scots College (02) 9391 7672.

Le Garcon

The Scots College's Nick Ward and Bailey Thomas contemplate a misfit boy's place in the world, and confronting the things we don't want to confront. In French/Black & White

Flying in Circles

International Grammar School's Ziyad Norhisham and Rex Woods create a world which harkens back to simpler times, to when everything was alright. And how to get back.

Anti Citizen

The Scots College's Mitchell Leslie presents a dystopic future with mind control, oppressive masked police and deserted apartment buildings.

For You

The Scots College's Angus Chrichton tells a heartfelt tale of his families heritage and his future.

Entry Rules/Terms and Conditions

The Scotsfest Short Film Festival will be held on Thursday the 19th November 2015, in the Scots College Main Audiotium, Victoira Road, Bellevue Hill. Guests are asked to arrive from 5:40pm with program commencing at 6:30pm.
Food and drinks will be sold on the evening.
Entry is free. For more information, please contact The Scots College (02) 9391 7672.

  • Entries are limited to school students.

  • Submissions must be work that has been produced 2015.

  • The use of any copyright music, graphics and video must be achnowledged in credits.

  • Copyright for the submitted films remains the property of the student, however,

  • Copies of films will be uploaded to the tscdigitalmedia Vimeo page and a link provided to entrants.

  • Film entries may be additionally distributed or screend at the discretion of Scotsfest committee.

  • Films must be submitted in Quicktime 16:9

  • Submissions must have been produced in the last 18 months.

  • On occasion, film entries may be additionally distributed or screened at the discretion of ScotsFest committee.

  • HD films preferred on USB or DVD. Submissions in 4:3 may be cropped.
